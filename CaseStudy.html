E-Commerce Design

Redefining Digital Luxury for Moris

Bridging the gap between high-fashion editorial and seamless e-commerce utility.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Outcome

High-Fidelity Prototype

The Challenge

Moris is a boutique fashion brand known for its craftsmanship. However, their digital presence was cluttered, resembling a generic marketplace rather than a luxury house. This led to a high bounce rate on mobile devices where the "premium feel" was lost.

My Goal: Create a digital experience that feels like walking into a high-end store—minimalist, spacious, and focused on the product details—while ensuring the checkout process remains frictionless.

Design Language

Typography

Aa
Plus Jakarta Sans (Headings)
Aa
Plus Jakarta Sans (Body)

Color Palette (Amber Series)

Warm Amber tones to evoke luxury and natural fabrics.

The Solution

I adopted a "Content-First" approach. By removing heavy borders and unnecessary UI elements, I let the photography speak for itself.

    Sticky Navigation: Keeps cart and menu accessible without blocking content.

    Visual Hierarchy: Using serif fonts for emotional headers and sans-serif for functional data (price, size).

Food & Beverage

Triggering Appetite with Ovenza

Designing a visceral ordering experience that converts hunger into orders in under 60 seconds.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Focus

Conversion Optimization

The Challenge

Most food delivery sites feel transactional and cold. Ovenza wanted to capture the warmth of a brick-oven pizzeria digitally. The challenge was balancing high-fidelity textures (to make the user hungry) with site performance and a speedy checkout flow.

Design Language

Typography

Aa
Playfair Display (Headings)
Aa
Plus Jakarta Sans (Body)

Color Psychology (Red Series)

Red stimulates appetite; balanced with deep blacks.

Key UX Decisions

1. The "Yum" Factor: Instead of small thumbnails, I used large, top-down photography with shadows that make the pizza pop off the screen.

2. Simplified Customization: Pizza builders can be tedious. I designed a visual stepper that allows users to toggle toppings on/off visually, making the process gamified rather than a form-filling exercise.

Mobile App Design

Simplifying Health Data with EmFit

Transforming complex bio-metrics into actionable, glanceable insights for the everyday athlete.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Focus

Data Visualization

The Problem

Health apps often suffer from "Data Overload." Users are bombarded with charts, numbers, and notifications, leading to anxiety rather than motivation. EmFit needed a solution that felt powerful yet "quiet"—an app that respects the user's attention.

Dark Mode Aesthetics

Color Strategy (Emerald Series)

High contrast neon on black for outdoor visibility and battery saving.

Iconography

We used rounded, filled iconography to ensure touch targets felt substantial and friendly, avoiding thin lines that disappear in sunlight.

The Dashboard Solution

I structured the dashboard using a Card-Based Layout. This allows users to modularize their view. If "Heart Rate" is more important to a user than "Steps," they can rearrange the priority.

Instead of showing raw data tables, we used Radial Progress Bars. It allows a user to understand their daily progress (e.g., "75% complete") in a millisecond glance.
